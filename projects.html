1. Location prediction of Twitter Users

My thesis is focused on predicting the location of Twitter users at city level using only the contents of their tweets. Existing approaches are purely data-driven and require large training data sets of geo-tagged tweets. Furthermore, these approaches rely solely on social media features or probabilistic language models and fail to capture the underlying semantics of the tweets. My thesis addresses this weakness using a novel knowledge based approach that does not require any training data set. This approach uses Wikipedia, a crowd sourced knowledge-base, to extract entities relevant to a city. We score the relevance of each local entity with respect to the city, using the Wikipedia Hyperlink Graph. We predict the most likely location of the user by matching the scored entities of a city and the entities mentioned by users in their tweets.

Publication: Revathy Krishnamurthy, Pavan Kapanipathi (Joint First Author), Amit Sheth, Krishnaprasad Thirunarayan. Knowledge Enabled Approach to Predict the Location of Twitter Users. Extended Sematic Web Conference, 2015. (acceptance rate 23%)

2. Temporal Reasoning in Medical Data

In the summer of 2013, I completed a 3 months internship at ezDI. ezDI provides products that organize, store and analyze medical data in near real time and embed the results of the analytics into a hospital's clinical workflow. Computer Assisted Coding simplifies the task of transforming medical diagnoses and procedures to universally accepted code numbers by automatically generating codes from the evidence gathered from the various medical documents of a patient. To increase the accuracy of CAC, a method to identify temporality of medical concepts was required. I used Random Forest Classifier along with dependency relationship between words to identify explicit temporal expressions and the medical concepts associated with them. My work was included in the final product released in October 2014.

3. Traffic Analytics

Traffic congestions have become a major issue in many cities around the world with serious economic and social implications. City infrastructure in general (e.g., road network, water network, power grid) has been under constant pressure to serve the needs of its citizens. With more than half the world's population living in cities by 2030, the condition is going to get worse. At Kno.e.sis researches work on understanding city issues such as traffic problems to provide insights to decision/policy makes. There are many challenges in dealing with city data such as heterogeneity, volume, velocity, and veracity.

I worked on the data management part of this project by designing a scalable infrastructure for handing the sensor data in Mongo DB. The data includes speed, volume, occupancy, delay, travel time, link(a predetermined segment of a road is referred to as link) etc are available every minute. Apart from this, static information like route details, schedules, fares etc and event information (scheduled and active) with the location and time are also provided. I also designed a visualization interface that can be used by researchers, city authorities, or citizens to view trends in traffic data.

4. PREDOSE

PREDOSE is an inter disciplinary research project aimed at developing techniques to facilitate prescription drug abuse epidemiology, related to the illicit use of pharmaceutical opioids. As a part of this project, I assisted in the identification of sentiments from online blogs. The goal was to identify trending positive or negative sentiments associated with specific drugs with research interest.
